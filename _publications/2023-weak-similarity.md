An important paper of the HO GSOS programme, as it was the first time we attempted to implement a complex, so-called "operational" method, at the level of generality of higher-order abstract GSOS. I was not prepared for the level of complexity of Howe's method... but Henning was! Henning wrote the majority of this paper, which is why the authorship is contribution-based, for the first and only time during my Erlangen days.

The end result is quite satisfying, as it strips Howe's method to its bare essentials and alleviates the need to reinvent the wheel at every instance. This pattern, of lifting a well-known operational method to an abstract level, continues in follow-up work.

In addition, it is here that we introduce the important notion of bifunctorial relation lifting, which fully unlocked the possibility of relational reasoning on this level of generality.

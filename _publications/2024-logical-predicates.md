My first forray into the world of logical relations (of any kind), and our second "operational methods" paper of the HO Abstract GSOS programme. The ubiquity of logical relations, as basically the prime method for program reasoning with operational semantics, meant that such a reckoning was unavoidable. I was also deeply motivated to finally systematize this particular method, as I found logical relations to be, by-and-large, ad-hoc.

The end result makes me happy. Logical predicates can now boast an abstract-yet-accurate definition, a general construction (to obtain a logical predicate that can be used in proofs of e.g. termination) is given, along with a surprising, simple and neat proof principle. This work opened a brand new path for the programme, and I thus find this work particularly important.

Special thanks to Alessio Santamaria: it was our meeting at Boston that kicked things off.

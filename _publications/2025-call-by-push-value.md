Up to this point, all of the work on the higher-order abstract GSOS framework has mainly involved call-by-name languages. More precisely, even though the framework is known to be able to model the operational semantics of call-by-value languages, instantiating it in the naive, obvious way will not yield the correct, CBV variety of the needed program equivalences. This paper takes aim at this issue, and resolves it by switching the base universe of discourse to that of a 'multisorted' category. This allows one to model languages of varying complexity including call-by-push-value, which is the main contribution of the paper. The compositional operational methods follow directly from the existing theory.

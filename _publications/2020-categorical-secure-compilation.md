My first categorical paper, and my (self-motivated) entry to the world of Abstract GSOS. The core concept is that the notion of a map of GSOS laws happens to pretty nicely characterize secure compilers. I support this viewpoint through a series of examples, adapted from the literature.

There is no deep categorical theory developed in this paper. However, by limiting the scope of the secure compilation problem to the Abstract GSOS paradigm, and putting the ensuing natural notion of a compiler to the test, this work serves both as the beginnings and a blueprint of a potential theory of secure compilers. The upside of such a theory? Formally secure compilers without much effort. I hope to further develop these ideas from 2025 and on.
